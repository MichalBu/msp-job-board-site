Why every quote you get will be higher than you expect

Bookkeepers describe cleanup work as a mystery box: they can't see how bad the file is until they're inside it, and most owners won't hand over account access before signing. So practitioners quote high to protect themselves, and the real number "almost always costs much more once you dig in." The single thing that lowers your quote is knowing exactly what's wrong before anyone bills an hour.

How this estimator works

  • Practitioners in public bookkeeping forums (July 2026) consistently quote $2,000-$6,000 for a typical 1-year cleanup, with $5,000-$15,000 common for larger or messier files, and CPA-grade work billed at up to $250/hour.
  • Monthly bookkeeping floors quoted repeatedly: $200-$500/month minimum, scaling with transaction volume.
  • The pricing mechanic bookkeepers describe using: months of catch-up x an equivalent monthly rate, plus a 10-25% hassle premium; some quote $2-$10 per transaction instead. Never-reconciled books and payroll add real hours.
  • This tool applies those same published mechanics to your inputs. It is a range, not a quote, and not accounting, tax, or financial advice.
